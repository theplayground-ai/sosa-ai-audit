Part 1: The Core Principle
Voice-First: Speak, Don't Type
You have Wispr Flow. That means you never need to manually edit a file. You speak into the terminal, and Claude does the rest.
THE GOLDEN RULE
Never open a file to edit it manually. Tell Claude what you want changed, and let Claude make the edit.
This applies to CLAUDE.md, context files, skills, code, everything. If you're typing into a file by hand, you're doing it the slow way.
EXAMPLE 1: UPDATING YOUR CONTEXT
Instead of: Opening CLAUDE.md in an editor and typing
SAY THIS INTO THE TERMINAL:
"Hey, update my CLAUDE.md to include that our Costa Rica expedition is $5,200 per person for 7 days, and our partner there is the local sea turtle conservation team. Also add that Blake leads every trip personally."
Claude reads the file, makes the edit, and saves it. You never touch the file.
EXAMPLE 2: CREATING A NEW SKILL
Instead of: Creating a .md file and writing a prompt by hand
SAY THIS INTO THE TERMINAL:
"Create a new skill called expedition-email that generates a 5-email post-trip nurture sequence. It should read our expedition details from context, use Blake's voice, and include specific impact metrics for each partner NGO."
Claude creates the file at .claude/commands/expedition-email.md with the full prompt. Done.
EXAMPLE 3: BUILDING SOMETHING NEW
Instead of: Writing code or designing layouts yourself
SAY THIS INTO THE TERMINAL:
"Build me an expedition booking page that shows all our 2026 trips, their dates, pricing, and what's included. Match our SOSA website branding. Make it so people can click to apply for each trip."
Claude builds the entire page, serves it locally, and you see it in your browser. Want changes? Just speak them.
EXAMPLE 4: IMPROVING RESULTS
Instead of: Rewriting prompts or editing outputs manually
SAY THIS INTO THE TERMINAL:
"That email sequence was good but the tone is too corporate. Make it more raw and personal, like Blake is texting a friend. Also the impact numbers in email 3 should be more specific to what Care for Wild actually does."
Claude takes the feedback, rewrites the content, and saves the updated version. Iterate by talking.

Part 2: The Real Power
Custom Skills: Your AI Automations
Skills are reusable prompts that run entire workflows with one command. This is where Claude Code goes from assistant to operations team.
HOW SKILLS WORK (VOICE-FIRST)
1
Speak Your Idea
Tell Claude what you want to automate. Describe the workflow in your own words using Wispr Flow.
2
Claude Creates the Skill
Claude writes the skill file for you, saves it in the right place, and sets it up as a /command.
3
Say /command to Run
Speak or type /your-skill-name and Claude executes the entire workflow automatically.
4
Speak Improvements
"That was good but change the tone" or "add a section about pricing." Claude updates the skill.

Expedition Email Writer

One command generates a complete post-expedition email sequence customized per trip destination, with personalized impact metrics and next-trip teasers.

Say this to create the skill:
"Create a skill called expedition-email that generates a 5-email post-trip nurture sequence. It should read our expedition details from context, write a welcome home email, a photo gallery email for day 3, an impact report with real numbers for day 7, a next-trip early access email for day 14, and an alliance membership invite for day 30. Use Blake's voice, authentic not salesy, and include specific impact metrics per partner NGO."
Or copy-paste this skill file:
Copied!# Expedition Email Writer # Save to: .claude/commands/expedition-email.md Read context/expeditions.md for trip details. Read context/partners.md for NGO impact data. Generate a 5-email post-expedition drip sequence for the expedition specified in $ARGUMENTS: 1. Welcome home (send immediately) - Emotional, personal, "you're part of this now" 2. Photo + video gallery (day 3) - "Your trip in photos. Share with everyone." 3. Impact report with REAL numbers (day 7) - Exactly where their money went, species helped 4. Next trip early access (day 14) - Alumni get 48hr head start, specific trip details 5. SOS Alliance membership invite (day 30) - Inner circle, researcher access, impact voting Voice: Blake Moynes. Authentic, raw, personal. Never corporate. Never salesy. Like texting a friend who just lived something incredible with you.
Then run it:
/expedition-email South Africa

Content Vault Repurposer

The big one. Feed in ANY existing content (old expedition vlogs, podcast episodes, field footage, blog posts, even raw B-roll descriptions) and it generates fresh content for every platform. Perfect for unlocking years of sitting content.

Say this to create the skill:
"Build me a skill called repurpose. When I run it, I'll give it a transcript or content from an old video or post. It should generate an Instagram caption, a TikTok script with a 60-second hook, a Twitter thread, a LinkedIn post, a blog post of 800 to 1200 words optimized for SEO, a YouTube description with timestamps, and a newsletter snippet. Match SOSA's voice throughout. Urgent but warm, mission-driven, never preachy."
Or copy-paste this skill file:
Copied!# Content Vault Repurposer # Save to: .claude/commands/repurpose.md Input: $ARGUMENTS (transcript or content description) Take this content and generate optimized versions for EVERY platform: - Instagram caption (max 2200 chars, 5-10 hashtags) Hook in first line. Story in the middle. CTA at end. - TikTok script (60 sec: hook + story + payoff) First 3 seconds must stop the scroll. - Twitter/X thread (5-7 tweets, thread format) Lead with the most surprising fact. - LinkedIn post (professional conservation angle) Position SOSA as thought leader. - Blog post (800-1200 words, SEO optimized) Target keywords: conservation, expedition, wildlife - YouTube description + timestamps Include booking link and merch link. - Newsletter snippet (200 words, email-ready) Drive to expedition booking page. Voice: SOSA brand. Urgent but warm. Mission-driven. Never preachy. Never use the word "organic." Conservation is a lifestyle, not a lecture.
Then run it:
/repurpose [paste transcript from any old video]
This is how you unlock your content vault. Years of expedition footage, one command per piece, seven platform outputs each.

Content Vault Strategy: Unlocking Years of Footage

SOSA has years of expedition footage, interviews, field journals, and social posts sitting unused. Here's the voice-first workflow to turn all of it into fresh content:

Step 1: Tell Claude about your content vault
"I have years of expedition footage and content that I want to repurpose. I'm going to start feeding you transcripts and descriptions of old content. For each one, I want you to tell me what platforms it would work best on, what angle to take for each platform, and then generate the actual posts. Start with the most emotionally powerful moments first."
Step 2: Feed content in batches
"Here's a transcript from our South Africa expedition in 2024. We collared two rhinos and trained with anti-poaching rangers. Pull out the best moments and create a week's worth of content from this single trip."
Step 3: Build a content backlog
"Take all the content you've generated from our last 3 expedition transcripts and organize it into a 30-day content calendar. Mix expedition content, education posts, merch features, and partner spotlights. Make sure we're posting 4-5 times per week across Instagram, TikTok, and YouTube."
